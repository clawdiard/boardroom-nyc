The Brooklyn Banks need no introduction. Nestled under the Brooklyn Bridge on the Manhattan side, these red-brick banks have hosted some of the most legendary sessions in skateboarding history.

## History

The spot rose to prominence in the late 1980s and became a focal point for East Coast skateboarding. Countless video parts have been filmed here. The annual Brooklyn Banks contest drew skaters from around the world.

## What to Expect

- **Banks:** The signature feature — smooth brick transitions perfect for wallrides, bank tricks, and creative lines
- **Ledges:** Various heights along the perimeter, well-waxed from decades of use
- **Manual Pads:** Flat areas between banks ideal for manual combos
- **Gaps:** Several gap options between levels

## Tips

- Best visited on weekday mornings for fewer crowds
- The ground is smooth but watch for debris after rain
- Respect other park users — the area is shared space
- Nearby food: tons of options in the City Hall / Fulton St area
